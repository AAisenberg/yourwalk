## What We're Building

Path Picker is a simple iPad game where people choose between two photos of walking paths. By seeing which paths people prefer, we learn what matters most to Casey residents when walking.

The game shows **scenario context** above each photo pair - an icon and temperature (e.g., üåô 15¬∞ for nighttime, ‚òÄÔ∏è 21¬∞ for a pleasant day). This tells participants what conditions to imagine when choosing.

**Why scenarios?** Lighting matters at night but not during the day. Shade is pleasant during the day but irrelevant at night. By separating scenarios, we get cleaner data.

**Why 21¬∞ for daytime?** At 21¬∞, the weather is neutral - shade is pleasant but not essential. This removes heat bias and lets us see what people actually prefer about different paths (including factors like safety feel and aesthetics, not just shade).

## How to Identify Each Attribute

### Lighting (for Nighttime photos): Would this feel safe to walk at night?

| Level | What to look for in Street View |
|-------|--------------------------------|
| **Good** | Visible street lights, open sightlines, well-maintained residential streets, commercial areas with shopfronts |
| **Moderate** | Some street lights but spaced out, mixed visibility, typical suburban streets |
| **Poor** | Underpasses, tunnels, paths behind buildings, heavily shaded areas with no visible lighting, isolated paths away from streets |

**Note**: Even in daytime Street View, you can tell if a path would feel dark at night by looking for:
- Are street lights visible?
- Is it isolated or near houses/shops?
- Does it have overhead cover (underpass, dense trees blocking sky)?
- Would you feel comfortable walking here at 9pm?

---

### Shade (for Warm Day photos): Would this be comfortable on a warm day?

| Level | What to look for in Street View |
|-------|--------------------------------|
| **Good** | Mature tree canopy over path, shaded walkways, awnings or covered areas |
| **Moderate** | Some trees but patchy coverage, intermittent shade |
| **Poor** | Fully exposed path, no trees, wide open concrete/asphalt, car parks, paths along main roads with no vegetation |

---

### Accessibility (for ALL photos): Could someone with a pram or mobility aid use this easily?

| Level | What to look for in Street View |
|-------|--------------------------------|
| **Good** | Wide, smooth concrete or asphalt path, no obstacles, gentle grade, clear kerb ramps |
| **Moderate** | Decent surface but narrower, some minor cracks or uneven sections, typical footpath |
| **Poor** | Cracked/broken surface, very narrow, overgrown vegetation encroaching, missing kerb ramps, steps or steep grade, obstacles (poles, bins) blocking path |

Accessibility is relevant in both scenarios - it always matters whether the path is usable.

## Photo Requirements

### Technical Specs

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| Minimum size | 1200px on the shortest side |
| Format | JPG or PNG |
| Orientation | TBD (we'll test on iPad) - capture in landscape for now |
| File naming | `night_01.jpg`, `night_02.jpg` for nighttime set; `hot_01.jpg`, `hot_02.jpg` for hot day set |

### Framing Guidelines

- **Perspective**: Looking down the path from a walker's viewpoint (eye-level)
- **Consistent angle**: Try to keep similar framing across all photos
- **Path as subject**: The path/footpath should be the main focus, not buildings or vehicles
- **Horizon position**: Roughly similar across photos

### What to Avoid

- Identifiable people or faces (blur or crop out if unavoidable)
- Prominent business names or signage
- Parked cars as main focus (some in background is fine)
- Unusual or distracting elements (construction, accidents, etc.)
- Very wide panoramic shots - focus on the path ahead

### Location Variety

Try to include a mix of:
- Residential streets
- Commercial/shopping areas
- Parks and reserves
- Near schools
- Different suburbs within Casey (Berwick, Cranbourne, Narre Warren, Clyde, etc.)

## Street View Tips

### Capturing Screenshots

1. Navigate to Casey in Google Maps, switch to Street View
2. Position the view looking down a path/footpath
3. Zoom to a natural walking perspective
4. Take a screenshot (avoid the Google UI elements if possible)
5. Crop to remove any visible Street View controls or watermarks

### Finding Good Examples

- Use the map to identify different area types (parks, residential, commercial)
- Footpaths along main roads often show different shade/exposure levels
- Look for parks with walking paths for good shade examples
- Underpasses and tunnels for "poor lighting" examples
- Older suburbs may have more accessibility issues (cracked paths)
- Newer estates often have better paths but less tree cover

## Quick Reference: What Makes a "Good" Photo

A good photo for this game:
- **For nighttime set**: Clearly shows lighting quality + accessibility (ignore shade)
- **For warm day set**: Clearly shows shade/exposure + accessibility (ignore lighting)
- Looks like somewhere in Casey (suburban Melbourne)
- Has the path as the main subject
- Would prompt someone to think "I would/wouldn't want to walk here"
- Is visually distinct from the other photos in the set

A poor photo for this game:
- Attributes are ambiguous or hard to read
- Distracting elements pull focus from the path
- Too similar to another photo in the set
- Unusual or unrepresentative location
